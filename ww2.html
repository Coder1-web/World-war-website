Overview of World War II

World War II was a global conflict that lasted from 1939 to 1945, involving most of the world's nations. It was the most widespread and deadliest war in history.

Causes of the War

The war was primarily caused by the aggressive expansion of Nazi Germany under Adolf Hitler, along with the ambitions of Japan and Italy.

Major Battles

  • Battle of Stalingrad
  • Battle of Midway
  • D-Day (Normandy Invasion)

The Pacific Theater

The Pacific Theater was marked by intense naval battles, island-hopping campaigns, and the eventual use of atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

The European Theater

The European Theater saw significant battles like the Blitzkrieg, the invasion of Poland, and the fall of Berlin, culminating in the defeat of Nazi Germany.
